Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how the developer of Sector Clash (package identifier com.sectorclash.game) handles information when you use the mobile game. Sector Clash is currently a local, single-player campaign game against computer-controlled opponents.

1. Information stored only on your device

Campaign progress, rewards, cards, upgrades, items, settings, and the local pilot display name are stored on your device. Sector Clash does not require an account, authentication, cloud save, or a multiplayer profile. This local game data is not uploaded by the game. You can remove it by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling the app.

2. Analytics information collected

Sector Clash uses Google Firebase Analytics to understand game usage, stability, and performance. When Analytics is enabled, the following categories may be collected:

  • Approximate location: coarse region information derived by Google from a masked or truncated IP address. The game does not request precise location permission.
  • App activity: screen views and limited gameplay events or summaries, such as tutorial milestones, match start and completion summaries, reward or economy actions, and use of game features.
  • App information and performance: app version, platform or device category, operating-system information, startup and scene-load timing, and summarized frame-performance information.
  • Device or other identifiers: a Firebase installation or app-instance identifier used for analytics measurement.

Sector Clash does not use Analytics to collect your name, email address, account credentials, precise location, contacts, photos, microphone or camera content, raw touch coordinates, station or node identifiers, or free-form communications. Android Advertising ID collection is disabled. Analytics data is used only to measure usage and improve reliability and gameplay.

3. Your Analytics choice

Analytics can be turned off from the in-game Settings screen. Turning it off stops future Analytics event collection from the app and resets the local Analytics app-instance state. Turning it on again begins a new measurement state. Because Sector Clash has no user account, previously aggregated Analytics records may not be identifiable as belonging to a particular player.

4. Service providers and sharing

Google processes Analytics data as a service provider for the developer through Firebase and Google Analytics. Sector Clash does not sell personal information and does not share Analytics information with advertising networks or data brokers. The current closed-testing build contains no enabled advertising service and does not display ads.

5. Purchases

The current closed-testing build does not enable in-app purchases. No billing or purchase information is collected by Sector Clash in this version. This Policy will be updated before any purchase feature is enabled.

6. Retention and deletion

The Google Analytics property is configured for the shortest standard user-level and event-level retention period: 2 months. Google notes that this retention control does not apply to standard aggregated reports. Local game data remains on your device until you reset it, clear app storage, or uninstall the app. You may submit a privacy or deletion inquiry through the contact mechanism below; due to the absence of accounts or direct identifiers, the developer may be unable to locate previously aggregated Analytics data for an individual player.

7. Security and international processing

Analytics data is encrypted in transit using secure connections. Google may process information on infrastructure located outside your country. No transmission or storage method is completely secure, but reasonable technical and organizational safeguards are used.

8. Children

Sector Clash is intended for a general audience aged 13 and older. It is not directed to children under 13, does not knowingly request personal information from children, and is not presented as a children's app.

9. Changes to this Policy

This Policy may be updated when the app's features, SDK configuration, or legal obligations change. The effective date at the top of this page will be revised when changes are published.
